1 Process Improvement — DMAIC / OEE

A 4-month Six Sigma DMAIC project on the melamine tableware line (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025), sustained with a real-time Power BI control dashboard.

🏆Headline results: OEE 63% → 73% (+10pp) · daily output ~25,000 → ~30,200 pcs/day (+20%) · defect rate 15–20% → 9–14% (−6pp) · downtime −10%.

D · Define

Problem: OEE on the line was stuck at ~63%, with a high defect rate (15–20%) and daily output of ~25,000 pcs that fell short of demand.
Goal (SMART): raise OEE 63% → ≥70% and cut the defect rate below 14% within 4 months.
CTQ: OEE, defect rate, downtime, daily output. Scope: mixing → finished goods across 2 shifts & 70 machines (excludes layout changes & new-machine purchases).

M · Measure

Collected output per shift & per machine, defect types & counts, downtime duration + causes, machine parameters and raw-material batch quality — pulled from production logs, cleaned/structured with Python, then validated for consistency (MSA).

A · Analyze

Using Pareto (dominant defect types), fishbone/Ishikawa (4M: Man, Machine, Method, Material) and correlation analysis, three root causes emerged:

  1. Unstable machine temperature/pressure parameters drove the dominant defect.
  2. An imbalanced senior–junior operator ratio across shifts caused output variance.
  3. Quality variation in certain raw-material batches correlated with higher defects.

I · Improve

  1. Standardized machine-parameter settings + a start-of-shift verification checklist.
  2. Rebalanced the senior–junior operator mix across shifts.
  3. Early-warning quality inspection of raw-material batches before production.
  4. Data-driven adjustment of production scheduling & sequencing.

C · Control

  • Real-time Power BI dashboard tracking output, defects, downtime and OEE across 2 shifts & 70 machines — replacing manual reports.
  • SOPs for parameter settings & start-of-shift verification.
  • Routine audits of parameter compliance + weekly KPI reviews, with a PIC assigned per metric.

The data

Method

A Holt-Winters additive model (α=0.1, β=0.1, γ=0.1, tuned by backtest) was selected after comparing it against Naive, Seasonal-Naive, 3-month Moving Average, and a Linear Regression with trend, seasonal, and lag-12 features. Prediction intervals come from the empirical distribution of backtest errors at each horizon. All models were implemented from scratch in NumPy.

Dataset is an illustrative sample reflecting a melamine-tableware demand pattern (trend + Lebaran/year-end seasonality); the methodology is identical on real, sanitized factory data.

3 ERP Operations — SAP PP & MM

One complete cycle: raw-material shortage → procurement (MM) → production (PP) → finished goods into the warehouse — with T-codes, master data, movement types and the document chain. A reproducible hands-on walkthrough on an SAP S/4HANA sandbox; the document numbers shown are from a sample run.

Raw-material shortage→ Procure-to-Pay (MM)→ Plan-to-Produce (PP)→ Finished goods to warehouse

Business scenario

Demand for Melamine Resin MF-200 is rising this quarter. A stock check shows the key raw material Melamine Powder is insufficient for the plan. Task: run one full cycle — secure the raw material via MM, produce MF-200 via PP — keeping stock and the accounting document chain consistent.

Integration points: MM ↔ PP ↔ FI

  • MRP (MD02) links finished-good demand → component requirements → Purchase Requisition, accounting for the 21-day lead time of the imported material.
  • Movement 261 (GI to the order) automatically reduces the raw-material stock that Movement 101 (PO Goods Receipt) increased — one consistent stock chain.
  • Every movement triggers an automatic FI/CO posting (valuation class + account determination), so physical stock and financial value always stay in sync.
