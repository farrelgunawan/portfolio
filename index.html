Root cause 01

Operators adjusting machine parameters independently — no standardized settings per product type

Root cause 02

Inconsistent SOP compliance across shifts — process execution varied by operator and shift team

Root cause 03

Incoming raw material quality varied across batches from the same supplier

D Define

Problem scoping — high defect rate and below-target output

  • Defined scope: defect reduction and output improvement across 70 machines, 2 shifts
  • Set targets: defect rate below 15%, output consistently ≥30,000 pcs/day
M Measure

Established baseline metrics before any changes were made

  • Collected daily output, defect type, and downtime data per machine and per shift
  • Tracked machine parameter settings across operators to identify variation patterns
  • Logged raw material batch quality on arrival to correlate with defect spikes
A Analyze

Identified three primary drivers of defects and output loss

  • Parameter deviation correlated directly with defect spikes on specific machines
  • SOP non-compliance varied significantly between shifts
  • Batch-to-batch material variation triggered unplanned defect increases
I Improve

Four concrete interventions implemented, October 2024

  • Standardized machine parameters per product type — operators prohibited from adjusting independently
  • Implemented incoming material quality inspection before raw material enters production floor
  • Rescheduled production priorities based on available material quality — low-quality batches deprioritized
  • Rebalanced operator composition across shifts to distribute senior operators more evenly (see Project 02)
C Control

Monitoring systems to detect regression early

  • Built real-time KPI dashboard in Power BI — tracking output, defect rate, downtime, and OEE daily; adopted by management as primary monitoring tool
  • Process documentation updated and aligned with ISO 9001:2015 to formalize parameter standards and material handling procedures

Problem 01

12 of 17 senior operators concentrated in Shift 2 — Shift 1 systematically underperforming

Problem 02

Output gap of ~4,000 pcs/day between shifts was driving overtime to compensate for shortfall

Problem 03

No formal criteria for shift assignment — composition had drifted informally over time

  • 01

    Data-driven diagnosis. Mapped operator seniority against per-shift output data in Excel to quantify the imbalance. Identified that output variance tracked closely with senior-to-junior operator ratios.

  • 02

    Rebalanced operator composition. Moved 3 senior operators from Shift 2 to Shift 1, targeting near-equal distribution (8 vs 9). Selection based on machine familiarity and product type expertise.

  • 03

    Monitored output gap weekly. Tracked daily output per shift in Power BI for 4 weeks post-change. Shift 1 output rose to ~14,500 pcs/day; the inter-shift gap narrowed from ~4,000 to ~1,200 pcs.

  • 04

    Overtime eliminated. With daily totals now meeting target without compensatory OT, scheduled overtime was removed entirely from August 2024 onwards.

Problem 01
Raw material procurement (melamine powder, resin, hardener) handled manually via email and phone — no structured PO audit trail or approval workflow
Problem 02
Inventory levels tracked in disconnected spreadsheets — no real-time stock visibility between warehouse and production floor
Problem 03
Raw material quantities per molding batch calculated manually by supervisors — no standardized BOM, prone to human error and material waste
Problem 04
21-day import lead time for melamine powder not integrated into production scheduling — unplanned stoppages occurred when stock ran out

SAP MM — Materials Management

  • 01 Blueprinting & requirement gathering. Mapped the end-to-end raw material procurement flow with procurement, warehouse, and finance teams to align SAP configuration with actual business processes at the tableware plant.
  • 02 Organizational Structure configuration. Set up Company Code, one Production Plant, and three Storage Locations — raw materials, WIP, and finished goods — reflecting the physical warehouse layout.
  • 03 Material Master creation MM01. Defined master data for all raw materials — melamine powder, resin, hardener, colorant, and release agent — including unit of measure, valuation class, and reorder points per material.
  • 04 Vendor Master XK01 & Info Records. Registered local suppliers and melamine powder importers; mapped preferred vendors per material via Source List to enforce purchasing standards.
  • 05 End-to-end Procure-to-Pay implementation. Configured full PR → PO → GR → Invoice Verification flow with Movement Types 101 and 261. Integrated with FI for payment processing. Conducted UAT with procurement and finance users prior to go-live.

SAP PP — Production Planning

  • 01 Production process analysis. Mapped the full molding production flow — material preparation, compression molding, trimming & finishing, quality inspection, and packaging — as the foundation for PP master data and configuration.
  • 02 Work Center setup CR01. Defined work centers for each production station: Mixing & Preparation, Molding Press Lines (70 machines across 2 shifts), Trimming & Finishing, and Packaging — with standard capacity and operation times per station.
  • 03 Bill of Materials creation CS01. Built BOMs for each product category — plates, bowls, cups, and serving sets — defining the precise raw material composition (melamine powder, resin, hardener, colorant) per production batch.
  • 04 Routing CA01 & Production Version. Defined operation sequences and standard processing times per batch; linked each BOM and Routing into a Production Version per product type to standardize the manufacturing process.
  • 05 MRP execution MD01 & Production Order management. Configured and ran weekly MRP to generate Planned Orders with a 21-day import buffer for melamine powder. Converted to Production Orders CO01, executed Goods Issue to production, confirmed output via CO11N, and posted GR for finished goods. Delivered hands-on user training for daily PP transactions.
